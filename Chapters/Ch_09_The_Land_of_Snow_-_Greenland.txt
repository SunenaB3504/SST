The Greenland Dog is used to pull carts / sledges. Economic activities Hunting and fishing are the main activities. The Inuits are expert hunters. They hunt animals for their meat, skin and fat. They hunt sea animals such as seals, walruses and whales. Being skilled fishermen, the Inuits also catch fish. More than 90 per cent of the total catch is exported, mainly to North America and Europe. Some people also work in mines and in oilfields. Every year, lakhs of tourists visit Greenland. So, the Inuits also depend on tourism to earn money. Life of the people The Inuits came to Greenland hundreds of years ago. They have adapted < themselves to the harsh              Two girls dressed in traditional clothes   climate. They have learnt to make the best use of things available locally. They use seal skin to make clothes and boots. They wear thick, hooded jackets and high boots to stay warm. The people who live in the extreme north of the island build and live in snow houses during the winter months. These houses are called igloos. They are made of ice. The floor is covered with the fur of animals. In summers, these people live in tents called tou pigs. These are made from the skin of animals. People in the cities live in houses made of bricks and wood. With time, the way of life of the Inuits has changed. They have started using modern things — automatic rifles instead of harpoons to hunt sea animals and motorboats instead of traditional boats for fishing. In the past they were dependent on locally-available things. Nowadays they use goods brought in from other countries. Many people now live in cities which have schools, hospitals and clubs.
