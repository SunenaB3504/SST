Though latitudes and longitudes are imaginary lines, you will see them on maps and globes as if they actually exist. LATITUDES OR PARALLELS The lines of latitude run from east to west. Each one is a circle. They are also parallel to one another. This means that one line never meets the other. That is why they are also called parallels. The Equator is the longest parallel. Features of latitudes ¢ All are circles, except the North Pole and the South Pole. Greenwich, a place near London, is the starting place to number the longitudes. The longitude that passes through Greenwich is marked 0° and is called the Prime Meridian. All other longitudes are measured east or west of the Prime Meridian and are labelled ‘E’ or ‘W. For example, 20°E longitude is 20° east of the Prime Meridian. Similarly, 70°W longitude is 70° west of the Prime Meridian. A circle has 360 degrees. If we move away from the Prime Meridian, towards the east or the west, we reach halfway round the Earth at 180°.
