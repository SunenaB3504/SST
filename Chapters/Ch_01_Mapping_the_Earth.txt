We cannot see the entire Earth at one time. To study the Earth, we use globes and maps. Globes Have you seen a globe? —., It is a small model of the Earth. It shows where the water bodies and the land masses lie. But we cannot always use a globe to study the Earth. This is due to the following reasons. ¢ A globe is small and a lot of details cannot be shown on it. ¢ A globe cannot be made for a small part of the Earth. ¢ A globe shows only a part of the Earth at a time. ¢ A globe is difficult to carry around. Maps, on the other hand, do not have these disadvantages. MAPS A map shows the entire Earth or a part of the Earth. It is drawn on a flat surface, such as paper or cloth. Maps can be big or small in size. They are made to show big areas such as continents or countries, or small areas such as your state or city. Maps can be folded, rolled or even assembled as a book.     Gerardus Mercator was a famous cartographer. He was the first to publish e “collection of maps in the form of. a book, called an atlas. He made his maps by hand. Today, maps are made on computers using special software. Maps, however, have one big disadvantage. You know that the surface of the Earth is curved. And a curved surface cannot be shown correctly on a flat surface. Can you cover a ball (which has a curved surface) with a sheet of paper (which has a flat surface) without creasing it? No, you cannot! In most cases, however, a map is more useful than a globe in order to study the Earth. Types of maps There are different types of maps. ¢ Political maps show continents, countries, states and big cities. ¢ Physical maps show water bodies (oceans, seas, lakes, rivers) and landforms (mountains, plateaus, plains). ¢ Climatic maps show the climate of a region. ¢ Crop maps show the different types of crops cultivated in an area. Apart from these, there are many other types of maps. You will read about them in higher classes. 7
