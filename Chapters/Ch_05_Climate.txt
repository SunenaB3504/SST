Let us learn how. Weather is the state of the atmosphere in a small area at a given time and place. It may change every day or even several times during the day. For example, a sunny day may suddenly become cloudy. Or a calm day may suddenly become windy. On a particular day, it may be dry and hot in the morning but rainy and cooler in the evening. Changes in weather conditions lead to different seasons. In India, there are three main seasons — summer, Monsoon and winter.

Unlike weather, the climate of a place remains the same, year after year. So, the polar areas are always cold (cold climate) while the desert areas are always hot and dry (hot and dry climate). FACTORS THAT AFFECT CLIMATE The climate of a region is usually determined by the temperature of the air. The air temperature, in turn, is determined by the heat that region receives. The Sun is our main source of heat. It gives off the same amount of heat at all times. Yet, all places on Earth do not receive the same amount of heat. Some places receive very little heat while some receive a lot of heat. Why is it so? There are five main factors. Distance from the Equator Study the figure carefully. Near the Equator, the rays are vertical. They fall directly over a small area. As we move from the Equator towards the North Pole and the South Pole, the rays get slanting. They are now spread over a larger area. So, places near the Equator receive the most heat while places near the poles receive the least heat.   Let us compare two places — A and B. Place A is in the mountains and Place B is in the plains. Both places are at an equal distance from the Equator. ‘This means that both places must have nearly the same temperature. But this is not so. Place A is colder than Place B. This is because Place A is at a higher altitude (being in the mountains) than Place B (being in the plains). So we can say that at higher altitudes, it is colder. And at places closer to the sea level, it is hotter. The sea has a cooling effect on the climate of a place. Places that are near the coast have a moderate climate. Here, it is neither too hot nor too cold. This is because during the day, cool air from the sea moves over land. This is a sea breeze. Places that are away from the coast do not get sea breezes. Therefore, they have an extreme climate - either too hot or too cold. Let us compare two places — Chennai and Delhi. Chennai is located along the coast while Delhi is far away from the coast. So, Chennai gets cool sea breezes while Delhi does not. That is     why Chennai has a moderate climate throughout the year. On the other hand, Delhi is very hot in summer and very cold in winter. Winds affect the climate of the place they blow through. Winds blowing from hot areas increase the temperature (makes it hotter) of the place they blow through while those blowing from cold areas decrease the temperature (makes it colder). Let us take the example of Delhi. Hot winds that blow from Rajasthan in summer make Delhi hotter. Similarly, cold winds that blow from Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand in winter make Delhi colder. How much rainfall a place receives is determined by the amount of humidity. Higher the humidity, more the rainfall. Air in deserts has very low humidity and so these places receive very little rainfall. Thus, places where humidity is high receive more rainfall and are cooler. Places where humidity is low receive less rainfall and are hotter. Coastal areas have a moderate climate. See

Torrid Zone The region between the Tropic of Cancer and the Tropic of Capricorn is called the Torrid Zone. It is also known as the Tropical Zone. The direct rays of the Sun fall over this region all year round. So, it is very hot here throughout the year. Temperate Zone There are two temperate zones. ¢ The region between the Tropic of Cancer and the Arctic Circle is called the North Temperate Zone. ¢ The region between the Tropic of Capricorn and the Antarctic Circle is called the South Temperate Zone. The Sun is never overhead or direct in these regions. So, it is neither very hot Nor very cold here.            72), Tropic of “.
